A model's phase diagram showing all twelve of the classical two-player game types, denoted by the different colored regions. This slice of the model's three-parameter mixed $CN$ phase space is indexed by the mixed benefit-to-cost ratio $\beta/c$ and the payoff asymmetry $\alpha$. For two players with phase difference $\Delta \phi$, the mixed benefit is $\beta = \beta_0 [1 + \cos(\Delta \phi)]/2$. The joint benefit-to-cost ratio $B_0/c$ is fixed at $1.5$ for this slice. The legend displays the game type corresponding to each color. The white dots represent the $20$ potential phase differences used throughout the study, restricted to $\alpha \in [0,0.25,0.5,0.75,1]$.
